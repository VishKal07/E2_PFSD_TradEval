The All-Too-Common Quest

Predicting tomorrow's price with AI – while seemingly advanced, this approach often falls short due to fundamental market realities.

🌪️ Inherently Unpredictable

Stock prices are subject to near-infinite variables, making accurate point-prediction a fool's errand.

📈 The Overfitting Trap

Models frequently overfit to historical data, learning noise instead of signal, leading to poor out-of-sample performance.

⚖️ The Illusion of Accuracy

High accuracy metrics rarely translate to actual, sustainable profit after accounting for real-world frictions.

The Critical Shift: Asking a Better Question

🔮

The Old Question

"What will the stock price be tomorrow?"

This frames the market as a crystal ball to be gazed into. It's a passive, speculative approach focused on a single, unknowable outcome.

✈️

The Right Question

"How does my strategy behave under various market conditions?"

This frames the market as an environment to be navigated. It's an active, scientific approach focused on process, resilience, and risk.

Advanced Avionics: Machine Learning Toolkit

Sophisticated tools for comprehensive analysis, not prediction

Logistic Regression

A stable baseline for evaluating feature sensitivity.

Random Forest

Handles noisy data and complex non-linear patterns.

Support Vector Machine

Effective for directional movement prediction, robust to noise.

K-Means Clustering

Unsupervised learning for market regime detection (volatility, trending, sideways).

Kalman Filter

Statistical ML for smoothing noisy price trends and evaluating performance under volatility stress.

Hierarchical Attention Network (HAN)

Advanced deep learning for processing news events and generating event impact scores without manual labelling.

Important: TradeEval's strength lies in its comprehensive evaluation framework, not a single predictive model. These algorithms are tools within the platform to compare strategy performance under realistic market conditions.
